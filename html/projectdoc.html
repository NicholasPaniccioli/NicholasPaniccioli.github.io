Projects

A few months after I finished my last class at RIT I found myself itching to create a game. With some new found experience in JS I wanted to make something small in scale, yet still fun. I also knew I wanted to mess with random number generation so the idea of a number guessing game just made sense.

It started with creating a minimal viable product. Getting the basic mechanic to work and nothing else. Is the number random? Yes. Can the player Guess? Yes. Can they Win? Yes. And so on. Once those were done it was about the checks and balances; giving limits on guesses, no words, adding a higher or lower mechanic, etc. Testing and testing until the base game was complete! I had my simple game done, but for me simple wasn't enough.

After I had finished the game, I created a point system. Win the game, win a point. Then it turned to a store to buy different colored backgrounds. Then different points for less or more guesses. Which then turned into an entire achievements tab, let's reward the player not only for winning, but for also playing. For those that love to know it all, let's give them their statistics. And of course with each new mechanic and tab added, more and more testing was needed. With the latest update putting this game in a place I'm 100% happy with.

Outer Space Experience was a school project I worked on in March of 2020. The goal of the project was to create a visual experience using Canvas and the AudioContext API to allow a user to change multiple settings of what was being shown. I gave users the options to change the speed of the surrounding planets, the color, and direction of the visual audio.

The project is finished as of right now with no urgency for updates. My plans for the project however do include some updates to the layout and the audio waves because they aren't perfectly perpendicular. Other than that it's just going through and refreshing myself on AudioContext, Canvas, and seeing other ways to improve on the project.

Summed up this project dealt with HTML/CSS, JSON, AudioContext API, and Canvas.

During my first internship at VR Networking I was given plenty of work. However, it wasn't until I was given the task to design and layout a convention center that my game design skills would be utilized. The space was to be used on AltSpace for both VR and PC, and every moment of it was amazingly beautiful and excruciating. There was a lot of planning, concept art, breaking, modeling, placing, turning, colliding, testing, VR-ing? The idea is there. The point being, I put a tremendous amount of work into the world I created and I couldn't be prouder.

I was given a hollow shell, some assets, few directions, and a lot of personal freedom in Unity when it came to laying out. Making some concept art and going back and forth with my supervisor we found something we liked and I got to work. At first it was overwhelming, there's nothing here and I have to fill it with how much? But I chipped away at it, started with one small section and built out. At first a botanical garden, then some rest areas next to it, the booths, more areas and seats, and before I knew it the entire space was filled. Even after putting on my headset and seeing the world, I could not believe my eyes.

However it wasn't always easy, I was also responsible for optimizing the entire space. Going through the assets to minimize the amount of triangles and adjusting the images/materials to lower the amount of space taken. Creating prefabs of booths and foliage, making them easy to edit for future use. In fact, the entire space was crafted so that once the internship is over my supervisor can easily pick up the work where I left behind and easily edit every aspect.
